02 — Executive Summary

A system of record agencies actually want to open.

Independent agencies run on management systems built in a different era — heavy, slow to change, and hostile to the integrations that now define the business. Catalyst is a cloud-native AMS designed around the daily reality of a producer, a CSR, and an operations lead, with automation and carrier connectivity as first-class citizens rather than bolt-ons.

18 mo Target from MVP to general availability
3 Beachhead segments at launch
API-first Every capability exposed as a service

This document lays out the problem we're solving, the market we're entering, the product architecture, and a phased plan to get to revenue. It is a design artifact, not a spec — the goal is alignment on direction before we commit engineering to the build.

The thesis in one line: connect the policy lifecycle end to end, make the everyday tasks disappear, and let agencies grow without adding headcount.

03 — The Problem

Incumbents digitized the filing cabinet. Nothing more.

Today's leading systems were architected before the modern web. They store data well but move it poorly, and the cost of that friction lands on the people least able to absorb it — the staff of a 5-to-50 person agency.

  • Fragmentation

    Client data, policies, documents, and accounting live in separate silos that don't reconcile without manual re-entry.

  • Stale connectivity

    Carrier downloads arrive as overnight batch files; real-time quoting and servicing sit outside the system entirely.

  • No automation

    Renewals, follow-ups, and compliance checks are tracked in spreadsheets and memory, not in the workflow.

  • Punishing UX

    Dense, modal-heavy screens require weeks of training and still slow experienced staff down.

  • Closed platform

    Thin or paywalled APIs make it hard to add the modern tools agencies now depend on.

  • Costly switching

    Migration risk keeps agencies locked into tools they've already outgrown.

04 — Market Opportunity

A large, entrenched market ready to move.

The U.S. independent agency channel is fragmented, durable, and underserved by software that keeps pace with how agencies now sell and service. Incumbency is an advantage that has stopped compounding.

40k+ Independent P&C agencies in the U.S.
$5–7B Estimated annual agency-tech spend
2–3 Incumbents holding the majority of share

Where we start

Small-to-midsize independent P&C agencies (5–50 staff) that feel incumbent pain most acutely and can adopt without a services army.

Where we expand

Adjacent motions — benefits agencies, MGAs and wholesalers, and multi-location networks — reachable once the core system of record is proven.

Figures are directional planning estimates for this review, not audited market data.

05 — Product Vision & Principles

Design principles we will not trade away.

These are the commitments that keep the product coherent as it grows. Every feature decision is measured against them.

  1. P1

    One connected record

    Client, policy, document, and ledger are one graph. Enter data once; it's correct everywhere.

  2. P2

    Automate the routine

    The system does the follow-ups, the checks, and the busywork by default — staff handle exceptions.

  3. P3

    Open by default

    Every capability is an API. Integrations are a platform feature, not a professional-services project.

  4. P4

    Fast enough to trust

    Screens respond instantly. A tool experienced staff can fly through beats one that merely looks modern.

07 — Architecture & Technology

Modular Monolith built on Django, React & GCP.

Built to scale to thousands of concurrent users. Strict module boundaries, serverless autoscaling, and automated intake pipelines form our technological moat.

L1

React Frontend Shell

Modular workspaces resolving components cleanly; Webpack module federation support for independent feature streams.

L2

Django Core & Apps

Decoupled Python services running via local event triggers to prevent database coupling and preserve modular boundaries.

L3

AI Intake Pipeline

Unstructured emails and PDF documents parsed via Cloud Run and Document AI, populating database fields automatically.

L4

GCP Managed Infrastructure

Cloud Run serverless compute, Cloud SQL (PostgreSQL + pgvector for knowledge search), and Memorystore Redis.

08 — Integrations & Ecosystem

The agency's other tools, connected on day one.

An AMS lives at the center of an agency's stack. Catalyst ships with the connections that matter and an open API for everything else.

Carrier download

IVANS and direct carrier feeds for policies, endorsements, and claims.

Comparative raters

Push prefilled data to and pull quotes back from the rating tools agencies already use.

Payments

Client payments, financing, and trust-accounting-aware reconciliation.

E-signature

Send, track, and file signed ACORD and application forms without leaving the record.

Accounting

Two-way sync with general-ledger systems such as QuickBooks.

Open API & webhooks

A documented public API and event webhooks for anything not covered natively.

09 — Security & Compliance

Trust is the product. We build for it.

Agencies hold some of their clients' most sensitive data. Security and compliance are foundational requirements, addressed in the architecture rather than added before an audit.

Encryption
TLS in transit and AES-256 at rest, with per-tenant key isolation.
Access control
Role-based permissions, SSO/SAML, and enforced multi-factor authentication.
Auditability
Immutable audit trail on every record change, exportable for review.
Certification
SOC 2 Type II as a launch milestone; GLBA safeguards by design.
Data governance
Configurable retention, deletion, and residency to meet state DOI requirements.
Resilience
Automated backups, point-in-time recovery, and a documented DR plan.

10 — Implementation Roadmap

Ship a sharp core, then widen.

A phased plan that gets a usable system of record into design partners' hands early and expands based on what we learn from real books of business.

  1. Phase 1

    Foundation Q3–Q4 2026

    Client & policy core, documents, roles, and the API/event backbone. Private design partners.

  2. Phase 2

    Connected agency Q1–Q2 2027

    Carrier downloads, commissions & accounting, workflow automation, and first integrations.

  3. Phase 3

    General availability H2 2027

    SOC 2, analytics, self-serve onboarding, and migration tooling. Open for paid sale.

  4. Phase 4

    Expand 2028

    AI assistance, benefits and MGA motions, and marketplace partnerships.
